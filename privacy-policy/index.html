Cookies

To ensure that this web page works correctly, we sometimes place small text files onto your mobile devices, known as cookies.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files which often include a unique identifier that are stored on your device when you visit certain web pages. Cookies are useful because they allow a website to recognise a user's device. With its help, the website memorizes your actions and preferred settings (like your user name, language, font size and other preferred settings relating to display functions) during a longer time period, so it’s not necessary to enter them again on each visit to the page or when navigating between pages.

How do we use cookies?

We use cookies on some of our pages, which are not indispensable for the display of the webpage, but they will provide you with a more pleasant browsing experience. You can erase or block these cookies, but if you do so, some of the functions of the page may be disabled and it may not work as it was designed to. Cookies enable the storing of the following data:

  • the language of the browser
  • whether or not you have consented to our use of cookies on that page etc.

We use first-party cookies (such as the Google Analytics cookie). We have no access to or control over cookies used by these companies or third-party websites. We suggest you check the third-party websites for more information about their cookies and how to manage them. 

If you don’t want us to set cookies, please disable cookies in your browser. This will reduce the usability of the site significantly.

If you want to know more about Third Party Cookies, please see www.allaboutcookies.org
